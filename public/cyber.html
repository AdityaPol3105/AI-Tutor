1. Importance of Cyber Safety

The Internet is powerful but can also be risky. Cyber safety ensures your personal data and online activity remain secure.

  • Protects against identity theft and online fraud
  • Prevents financial loss and phishing scams
  • Maintains privacy on social media and emails
  • Teaches safe online behavior and digital etiquette

2. Creating Strong Passwords

Strong passwords are essential for protecting accounts and personal information.

  • Use at least 12 characters combining letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid common words or easy-to-guess info (like birthdays)
  • Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts
  • Use password managers to safely store complex passwords
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible

3. Recognizing Online Scams

Scammers try to steal money or personal info using tricks online. Learn to spot scams:

  • Phishing emails pretending to be banks or companies
  • Fake shopping websites or suspicious advertisements
  • Malicious links on social media or messaging apps
  • Impersonation of friends or authority figures

Advanced tips:

  • Hover over links to verify URLs
  • Use website reputation checkers before visiting unknown sites
  • Report scams to authorities or platform admins

4. Safe Social Media Practices

Social media is fun but can be risky if used carelessly.

  • Keep profiles private and share information selectively
  • Do not accept unknown friend requests
  • Think before clicking links or downloading files
  • Use two-factor authentication for added security
  • Monitor your digital footprint; what you post stays online
  • Be aware of cyberbullying and how to report it

5. Safe Internet Usage

Follow these best practices to stay safe online:

  • Keep your software and antivirus updated
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks
  • Backup important files regularly
  • Log out from accounts on shared devices
  • Use secure websites (HTTPS) for transactions
  • Understand privacy settings on apps and browsers
